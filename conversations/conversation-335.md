## Key Points
- what qualifies as hardship for financial payment “Hardship” for financial payment typically refers to a situation in which a person cannot reasonably meet financial obligations due to unexpected or unavoidable circumstances.
- What qualifies as hardship may vary depending on the lender, institution, or program, but common examples include: ### ✅ **Qualifying Hardship Situations** 1.
- **Job loss or reduced income** - Layoff, furlough, or significant reduction in work hours.
- **Medical emergencies or illness** - Large medical expenses not covered by insurance or inability to work due to illness.
- **Disability** - Temporary or permanent disability that affects your ability to earn income.
- **Death of a family member** - Loss of a wage-earning spouse or dependent-related expenses.
