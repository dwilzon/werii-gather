## Key Points
- difference between compassion empathy and sympathy Here’s a clear way to see the difference between **compassion**, **empathy**, and **sympathy**: --- ### **1.
- Sympathy – “I feel *for* you”** - **Definition:** An acknowledgment of someone else’s hardship or pain, usually accompanied by pity or sorrow.
- - **Focus:** The other person’s suffering, seen from the outside.
- - **Example:** If a friend loses a loved one, you might say, *“I’m so sorry for your loss.”* - You care, but you don’t necessarily share or deeply enter into their emotional experience.
- Empathy – “I feel *with* you”** - **Definition:** The ability to understand and share the feelings of another person—almost as if you are stepping into their shoes.
- - **Focus:** Entering into the other person’s emotional world.
