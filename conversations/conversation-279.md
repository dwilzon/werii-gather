## Key Points
- What is the term for "going through the motions" A common term for **"going through the motions"** is: ### **"Performing mechanically"** – This describes doing something without emotion, enthusiasm, or thought—just out of habit or obligation.
- Other similar expressions or phrases include: - **"Phoning it in"** – Doing the bare minimum, without effort or genuine engagement.
- - **"Autopilot"** – Functioning automatically without conscious thought or intent.
- - **"Rote behavior"** – Repetitive actions performed without deep thought, often memorized or habitual.
- - **"Checking the boxes"** – Completing tasks for the sake of formality, not with purpose.
- - **"Going through the paces"** – Completing expected steps without real involvement.
