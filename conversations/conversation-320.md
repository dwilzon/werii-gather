## Key Points
- what is moral filth The phrase **“moral filth”** is usually used in a figurative or ethical sense rather than a literal one.
- It describes attitudes, behaviors, or influences that are considered spiritually, ethically, or socially corrupting — things that degrade a person’s character or values.
- ### Common contexts where it appears: - **Religious/Scriptural usage** – For example, James 1:21 in the Bible (often translated as “get rid of all moral filth”) uses it to mean sinful tendencies, corrupt desires, and harmful speech or actions that go against G
- - **Cultural commentary** – Writers and speakers sometimes use it to refer to media, entertainment, or cultural norms they believe promote dishonesty, cruelty, greed, lust, or other vices.
- - **Personal ethics** – Someone might call gossip, cruelty, exploitation, or manipulation “moral filth” if they see those as contaminating a person’s inner life.
- ### Core idea: It’s metaphorical — “filth” here means **pollution of the heart, mind, or conscience**.
