## Key Points
- what is the name of the snake that eats its tail The symbol of a snake eating its own tail is called the **Ouroboros**.
- It originates from ancient Egyptian iconography and was later adopted by the Greeks.
- The Ouroboros represents **cyclical renewal**, **infinity**, and the **eternal return** — the idea that life, death, and rebirth are all interconnected in an endless loop.
- It’s often used in philosophy, alchemy, and mythology to symbolize the **unity of beginnings and endings** — that creation arises from destruction, and vice versa.
