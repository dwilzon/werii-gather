## Key Points
- who are the root spoken about in roman’s 11 In Romans 11, Paul uses the imagery of an **olive tree** to describe God’s people and His covenant promises.
- The **“root”** in this picture refers to the **foundational source of blessing, life, and covenant connection with God**.
- There are two main ways this has been understood: ### 1.
- The Root as the Patriarchs / God’s Covenant Promises Many interpreters see the root as **Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob**, or more broadly the covenant promises God made to Israel.
- Paul even points to this idea later in the chapter: *“As regards election, they are beloved for the sake of their forefathers”* (Romans 11:28).
- In this sense, the Jewish people remain connected to the root because of God’s promises to the patriarchs.
