## Key Points
- what does it mean doing justice The phrase **“doing justice”** means **acting in a way that is fair, right, and aligned with moral or legal principles**.
- It’s more than just following the law—it’s about actively ensuring that people are treated rightly and that wrongs are corrected.
- Here are a few angles to understand it: --- ### **1.
- Ethical / Moral Meaning** To *do justice* means to act rightly toward others—especially in situations where someone is vulnerable, oppressed, or wronged.
- - Example: *Speaking up for someone who’s being mistreated is doing justice.* It implies standing on the side of what’s right, even when it’s inconvenient.
- Legal Meaning** In law, *doing justice* means applying laws fairly and without bias.
